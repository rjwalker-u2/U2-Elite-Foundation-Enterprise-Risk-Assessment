U2 Elite Foundation — Incident Response Plan (IRP)

Version 1.0
Effective Date: January 2025

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Incident Response Plan (IRP) is to provide a structured and repeatable approach for identifying, reporting, analyzing, and responding to cybersecurity incidents that may affect U2 Elite Foundation systems, data, or operations. This plan ensures coordinated actions that minimize impact and support timely recovery.

2. Scope

This IRP applies to all:

Staff

Coaches

Volunteers

Contractors

Third-party partners handling U2 Elite Foundation data

Covers incidents involving:

Google Workspace

Registration portal

Payment systems

Event devices

Social media accounts

Internal communication tools

Participant or donor data

3. Definitions

Security Incident: Any event that could compromise confidentiality, integrity, or availability of systems or data. Examples include:

Phishing or account compromise

Data exposure or unauthorized access

Lost or stolen devices

Malware or ransomware

Misconfigured cloud storage

Fraudulent payment activity

Social media account takeover

4. Incident Response Team (IRT)
4.1 Roles & Responsibilities

Incident Response Lead (IR Lead)

Coordinates all incident response activities

Communicates with leadership

Documents incident timeline and actions

IT Support / Technical Lead

Performs technical remediation

Collects logs, evidence, and verifies containment

Staff or Volunteer Reporting Party

Immediately reports suspected incidents

Assists with initial information gathering

Executive Leadership

Approves major response actions

Handles external communication if needed

5. Incident Response Phases
5.1 Phase 1 — Identification

Determine whether an event qualifies as a security incident.
Indicators include:

Suspicious login alerts

Unexpected file sharing

Reports of phishing emails

System outages

Unauthorized changes to data

Action:

Immediately report to the IR Lead

Begin logging timestamps and details

5.2 Phase 2 — Containment

Limit further damage by isolating affected systems or accounts.

Short-term containment actions:

Disable compromised accounts

Remove public file-sharing links

Disconnect affected devices from the network

Long-term containment actions:

Change passwords

Apply patches or configuration fixes

Block malicious IPs or URLs

5.3 Phase 3 — Eradication

Eliminate the root cause of the incident.
Examples:

Removing malware

Deleting unauthorized accounts

Cleaning infected files

Reconfiguring permissions

5.4 Phase 4 — Recovery

Restore normal operations while ensuring systems are secure.
Actions may include:

Restoring from backups

Re-enabling accounts

Monitoring for unusual activity

Validating system integrity

5.5 Phase 5 — Lessons Learned

Within 72 hours of resolution, the IR Team must:

Conduct a post-incident review

Identify what worked and what didn’t

Update policies or controls as needed

Document the full incident report

This strengthens future readiness and compliance.

6. Reporting Procedures
6.1 Immediate Reporting

Any user who suspects a security incident must report it immediately to:

The Incident Response Lead

Organizational leadership

Preferred methods:

Email

Phone call

Direct in-person notification

6.2 Information to Include

Description of the issue

Time it occurred

Systems involved

Any actions already taken

7. Communication Plan

Communication during incidents must be controlled to avoid misinformation.

Internal communication: IR Lead → Leadership → Affected staff

External communication: Only executive leadership or authorized designee

Regulatory notifications: If participant or donor data is affected, follow state breach notification laws

8. Evidence Preservation

All logs, screenshots, emails, and device information relevant to the incident must be preserved.
Do NOT delete, modify, or reset anything before IR Lead approval.

9. Policy Review

This Incident Response Plan must be reviewed annually or following any major security incident.

10. Acknowledgment

All staff, coaches, volunteers, and contractors must acknowledge and understand this IRP as part of their cybersecurity responsibility.

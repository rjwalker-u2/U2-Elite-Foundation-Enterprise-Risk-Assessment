U2 Elite Foundation — Access Control Policy (ACP)

Version 1.0
Effective Date: January 2025

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Access Control Policy (ACP) is to ensure that only authorized individuals have access to U2 Elite Foundation systems, applications, and data. This policy establishes guidelines for managing user access, authentication, and account permissions to protect participant information, donor data, internal documents, and other critical assets.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

Employees

Coaches

Volunteers

Contractors

Any individual accessing U2 Elite Foundation systems or data

Covers:

Google Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Docs)

Registration platforms

Payment systems (Stripe, QuickBooks)

Event devices

Social media accounts

Internal communication tools

3. Access Management Principles
3.1 Least Privilege

Users are granted the minimum level of access required to perform their job responsibilities.

3.2 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Access is assigned based on the individual’s role within the organization (e.g., Coach, Volunteer, Administrator).

3.3 Separation of Duties

Where possible, system responsibilities are divided to reduce the risk of misuse or fraud.

3.4 Authorized Access Only

Only approved users may access systems or data. Shared accounts are discouraged unless strictly necessary and documented.

4. User Account Management
4.1 Account Creation

Accounts must be requested through an authorized manager.

Accounts must be created using U2 Elite Foundation naming conventions.

4.2 Account Changes

Access must be updated when a user’s role changes (e.g., volunteer to coach, staff reassignment).

4.3 Account Termination

Accounts must be disabled within 24 hours when a user leaves the organization.

Access to shared drives or systems must be removed immediately.

5. Authentication Requirements
5.1 Password Standards

All passwords must:

Be at least 12 characters

Contain uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols

Not be reused across personal accounts

Be changed when compromised

5.2 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA must be enabled for:

Google Workspace

Payment systems

Social media accounts

Any system containing sensitive data

6. Access Reviews
6.1 Quarterly Access Reviews

Supervisors must review access permissions every quarter to ensure:

Users still require their current access levels

No inactive accounts exist

No unauthorized access was granted

6.2 High-Risk Systems

Systems containing donor or participant data must undergo access review at least monthly.

7. Privileged Access Management
7.1 Administrative Access

Administrative access is strictly limited to individuals who require it for operational needs.

7.2 Logging & Monitoring

Administrative actions must be logged and periodically reviewed to ensure appropriate use.

7.3 Elevated Access Requests

Requests for elevated access must:

Be documented

Include justification

Be approved by organizational leadership

8. Use of Personal Devices (BYOD)

Personal devices may be used ONLY if:

They are updated

They use strong device passwords

Screen lock is enabled

Organizational data is not stored outside approved apps

9. Incident Response for Unauthorized Access

Any suspected unauthorized access must be reported immediately.
Actions taken may include:

Account suspension

Password reset

Investigation of logs

Notification of leadership

10. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or after major technological or organizational changes.

11. Acknowledgment

All users must acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to follow the Access Control Policy.

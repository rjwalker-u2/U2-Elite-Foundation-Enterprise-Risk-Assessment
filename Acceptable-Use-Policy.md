U2 Elite Foundation — Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

Version 1.0
Effective Date: January 2025

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to outline the acceptable and responsible use of U2 Elite Foundation’s information systems, devices, applications, and data. This policy ensures that all staff, coaches, volunteers, and contractors understand their responsibilities in maintaining a secure and compliant technology environment.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

Full-time and part-time staff

Coaches and volunteers

Contractors or third-party service providers

Any individual accessing U2 Elite Foundation systems, data, or devices

Covers usage of:

Google Workspace

Email and communication platforms

Event laptops/tablets

Registration portals

Payment systems

Social media accounts

Personal devices used for organizational work

3. Acceptable Use Requirements
3.1 Authorized Access Only

Users must access systems and data only for official organizational purposes. Shared accounts without permission are prohibited.

3.2 Password Security

Use strong, unique passwords

Do not share passwords with anyone

MFA must be enabled where available

3.3 Device Usage

Keep devices updated

Report lost/stolen devices immediately

Do not install unauthorized software

3.4 Email and Communication

Do not click unknown links or attachments

Report phishing attempts immediately

Use professional language in all communications

3.5 Cloud Storage

Store only organizational files in shared drives

Do not share links publicly unless approved

Restrict sensitive information to authorized users

3.6 Internet Usage

No illegal, inappropriate, or harmful content

No use of organizational systems for personal gain

4. Prohibited Activities

Users may not:

Attempt to bypass security controls

Access data they are not authorized to view

Install unapproved applications

Use personal email for organizational data

Share organizational information outside approved systems

5. Data Protection

Users must take reasonable steps to protect:

Participant information

Donor information

Internal documents

Financial/payment data

This includes locking screens, securing devices, and maintaining confidentiality.

6. Reporting Requirements

Users must report the following immediately:

Phishing or suspicious emails

Lost or stolen devices

Unauthorized access

Misconfigurations or exposed data

Any potential security incident

Reports should be sent to the designated security contact or organizational leadership.

7. Violations & Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:

Revoked system access

Disciplinary action

Removal from volunteer/staff roles

Potential legal consequences depending on severity

8. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or after any significant organizational or technological changes.

9. Acknowledgment

All users must acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agreed to this Acceptable Use Policy.
